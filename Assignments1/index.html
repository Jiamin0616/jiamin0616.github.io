Endless Summer - Interactive Experience Research Project

By Jiamin

Research question

Q1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

  • The first thing that catches my eye is the visual design of the website. The site uses a dynamic video background and 3D visual effects. Players explore the story through a scrolling interactive format. The website uses a high contrast color scheme throughout, bright and colorful at the same time full of vitality. Create a natural atmosphere with a beachside summer feel.

Q2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • 1. Access the website by clicking the provided link.
  • 2. Resize the website interface by dragging the edges.
  • 3. Initiate the process by clicking the "Start" button.
  • 4. Review the introduction and proceed by clicking "Continue."
  • 5. Use mouse dragging to navigate further.
  • 6. Scroll down to explore the website content, triggering page animations and video displays dynamically.
  • 7. Parallax animations are automatically activated during scrolling.
  • 8. Videos on the page will automatically play or pause depending on the scroll position.
  • 9. Locate the hidden feature and activate it to trigger a dynamic effect.
  • 10. Press the designated button to experience the poetic interlude.
  • 11. Optimize the speaker settings for an enhanced experience.
  • 12. Click and hold the button to fully immerse in the audio experience.
  • 13. Hovering over images with the mouse will generate visual feedback.
  • 14. Toggle background music and sound effects on or off using the dedicated button.
  • 15. Select entries in the navigation menu to swiftly navigate to corresponding sections of the page.
  • 16. Collapse the menu panel by clicking the "Close" button.

Q3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

  • I dedicate the most time to appreciating the poetic interludes of the beach. The content centers on summer vitality, exploration, and freedom, resonating with the brand's emphasis on adventure and a high-performance lifestyle. Additionally, the content is rich and vivid, seamlessly integrating elements of sports, entertainment, and nature.

Q4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

  • The most prevalent type is the sliding bearing, and users can access story details by clicking the corresponding button. Scrolling through the content reveals each node as a story chapter. On some pages, there may be a slight delay between scrolling actions and mouse interactions. In the poetic interlude section of the video, a low frame rate is employed, with text descriptions fading in and out for artistic effect.

Q5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

  • The website's primary objective is to establish a dialogue with users through a series of immersive designs and interactive behaviors. The brand's ethos, as embodied in its name, is predicated on the values of freedom, fearlessness, energy, and exploration. The website has been designed to elicit a strong emotional response in users, thereby increasing interaction rates and engagement, and enhancing the perceived strength of the brand image.

Q6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

  • Initially, the designers employed dynamic images, sounds, and scrolling animations to evoke users' imagination of summer, sunshine, the sea, and freedom. Not only that, but the video transitions were also very smooth and delicate, and the page transitions utilized fade-in/fade-out methods. Moreover, the integration of mouse-over interactions was accompanied by subtle animation feedback, providing a seamless and intuitive user experience. The design of the loading animations and progress bars adopted a soft and light form to maintain users' immersion.

Q7.What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

  • I think "endless summer" is about a deep experience. The characteristics of this website can inspire users' empathy, with emotional marketing as the core. The content of the site creates more of a sense of storytelling. A lifestyle to aspire to, rather than showcasing product details and technology. More like a film or art exhibition than an e-commerce-style product site, it attracts groups with a desire for exploration and artistic aesthetics. As a result, users form a long-term memory and want to stay on the page for a while. Therefore, the user's browsing time should be in the ten to fifteen minutes, ranging from short-term will not revisit the situation. It is one of the sites that will leave a long-term effective memory, although it will not be browsed frequently.

Q8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

  • First, the site is meant to be a space for exploring, not a place to browse for information about products. The website has a relaxing and enjoyable atmosphere. The brand wants people to have a sensual experience, not just read information. The website doesn't have clear navigation, which makes it feel like you're exploring. This creates an added sense of mystery that makes you want to come back. Every click reveals something new.
  • Secondly, the website uses a large area of white space. Slow animation rhythm, short language, these give users the opportunity to savor and understand. At the same time, the story is special, which is the romantic memory of the summer. This gives users the opportunity to revisit the site.
  • Furthermore, the incorporation of a light and shadow effect feedback mechanism upon mouse clicks serves to enhance user engagement and interaction. This enhances the user's sense of engagement and involvement, fostering a desire to return to the platform. Finally, the player's ability to access non-linear memory enables the interpretation of content from diverse perspectives, thereby enhancing the overall experience.

Q9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

  • The website uses short films. The main visual design is to make the "reality" weaker and the "dream sense" stronger by using low-frame rate video and slow-paced text. The video uses the lens language to tell the story, emphasizing feelings rather than information. It's more like a timeline than a movie, where each scroll is a new story. At the same time, the music and images work together to create a more immersive experience for the user.
  • It's also like a fun interactive installation experience. The user is not passively watching but is propelled by interaction. With every click and action, the user feels a sense of control. It can be said that it is an art installation that is easy to operate online.
  • At the same time, the website is also very fashionable and novel in terms of page design. Some magazine styles are referenced here. Like large areas of white space, minimal text, advanced color matching and high contrast composition.

Q10. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

  • The website lets users participate in a way that isn't linear or focused on specific functions. It does this by using an immersive story, sensory activities, and emotional rhythm.
  • The website guides users deeper by using atmosphere, rhythm, and pictures. The whole experience is immersive. Users don't have to think too much or look for answers. Just swipe the screen and the story unfolds. Although there is only one movement, it controls all the rhythm, including images, music, and words. Each pause in the music and words matches the rhythm of the images, which evokes the reader's emotions.
  • The site guides the user smoothly through the experience with mouse scrolling and page response. This type of interaction emphasizes openness and memorability. The user doesn't need to return often, but leaves a lasting memory through audiovisual art. This can be transformed into a brand awareness that users can re-enter every summer in the future.

Q11. What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

  • This website has inspired me. Until now, I had always felt that immersive video was one-way, that the video communicated to the user rather than to each other. In this experience, I felt the charm of interactivity. As a participant, my feelings and participation are more important than observation and analysis. I can feel the resonance. It's a delicate sensory experience.

Q12. What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

  • At first, I wasn't sure where to click. There are no obvious buttons or prompts. This can cause confusion. I'm always afraid of missing something, of not having a guiding "something." Then there are the sound effects. The autoplay music that starts when you turn it on surprised me. At the same time, there's a feeling that you have been listening to the same melody, and it's a little boring. The musical logic is lacking. In the end, the site used mostly linear browsing and hidden navigation. If you jump to watch or refresh in the middle of an easy-to-find way, you might miss it.

Q13. What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

  • One of the things I'm most pleased with is the visual design of the site. The main colours are dark blue and gold. It gives an immersive, clean, dreamy and natural vibe. The video uses beautiful shots of things like waves, sunshine, and the beach. The low frame rate faux-film filter creates a summer movie atmosphere. Like a wonderful and romantic dream.
